Understanding Air Pollutants

Air quality is affected by various pollutants, each with different sources and health impacts. Below are the major pollutants we monitor:

PM2.5

Fine particulate matter smaller than 2.5 micrometers. These tiny particles can penetrate deep into the lungs and even enter the bloodstream, causing respiratory and cardiovascular issues.

PM10

Coarse particulate matter between 2.5 and 10 micrometers. These particles can cause irritation to the eyes, nose, and throat, and may worsen existing respiratory conditions.

Ozone (O₃)

Ground-level ozone forms when pollutants from vehicles and industry react in sunlight. It can trigger asthma attacks, reduce lung function, and cause respiratory inflammation.

Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂)

A gas produced during combustion processes, especially in vehicles and power plants. It can irritate airways and increase susceptibility to respiratory infections.

Sulfur Dioxide (SO₂)

A gas produced from burning fossil fuels containing sulfur. It can cause respiratory symptoms and make breathing difficult for those with asthma.

Carbon Monoxide (CO)

A colorless, odorless gas produced by incomplete combustion. It reduces oxygen delivery to the body's organs and can be fatal at high concentrations.
